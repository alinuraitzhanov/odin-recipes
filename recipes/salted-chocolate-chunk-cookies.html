Salted Chocolate Chunk Cookies

Description

A bakery-style cookie featuring crisp, golden edges, a perfectly chewy center, and massive pools of melted dark chocolate. The addition of flaky sea salt right out of the oven cuts through the intense sweetness, balancing the rich molasses notes of the brown sugar.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly
  • 1/2 cup granulated white sugar
  • 1/4 cup packed dark brown sugar
  • 1 large egg (at room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1.25 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 6 oz high-quality dark chocolate, roughly chopped into chunks
  • Flaky sea salt for finishing

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the melted unsalted butter, white sugar, and dark brown sugar until the mixture is smooth and no lumps remain.
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract to the sugar mixture. Whisk vigorously for about 1 minute until the batter becomes pale, slightly thickened, and shiny.
  3. In a separate smaller bowl, stir together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and fine sea salt.
  4. Pour the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients. Use a wooden spoon or rubber spatula to fold the mixture together just until a dough forms. Do not overmix.
  5. Gently fold in the chopped dark chocolate chunks, ensuring they are distributed evenly throughout the dough.
  6. Cover the bowl and chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  7. While the dough chills, preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  8. Scoop out large balls of dough (about 2 tablespoons each) and place them on the baking sheet, leaving roughly 2 inches of space between them.
  9. Bake for 10-12 minutes until the edges are golden brown, but the centers still look slightly soft and underbaked.
  10. Remove from the oven and immediately sprinkle the top of each hot cookie with a pinch of flaky sea salt. Let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.
