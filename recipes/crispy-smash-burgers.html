Crispy Smash Burgers

Description

The ultimate fast-casual style burger made right at home. By smashing the beef tightly against a screaming-hot surface, you force a rapid Maillard reaction that creates an incredibly flavorful, deeply seared, crispy outer crust while keeping the inside juicy.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio is essential)
  • 4 brioche burger buns
  • 4 slices American cheese
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (for toasting buns)
  • Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon yellow mustard
  • Sweet pickle relish to taste

Steps

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 4 oz each) and gently shape them into loose, round balls. Do not overwork the meat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, ketchup, mustard, and pickle relish to create the burger sauce. Set aside.
  3. Heat a heavy cast-iron skillet or griddle over high heat until it is smoking hot.
  4. Melt the butter in the pan and place the burger buns face-down. Toast until golden brown (about 1-2 minutes), then remove and set aside.
  5. Place the beef balls onto the dry, ungreased hot skillet, leaving ample space between them.
  6. Using a heavy, flat spatula, smash each ball flat into a very thin patty. Press down firmly for 10 seconds to ensure total surface contact. The edges should look thin and jagged.
  7. Season the patties generously with salt and pepper. Let them cook undisturbed for about 2 minutes until the edges are dark brown and crispy.
  8. Use a sharp metal spatula to scrape under the patty (keeping the crust intact) and flip it over.
  9. Immediately place a slice of American cheese on each patty. Cook for 1 more minute until the cheese melts and the bottom is cooked through.
  10. Spread the burger sauce onto the toasted buns, stack the patties, and serve hot.
